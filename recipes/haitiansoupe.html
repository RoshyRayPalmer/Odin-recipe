Haitian pumpkin soup

  • Preparation

    20 min

  • cooking

    45 min

  • Portions

    8

Soup joumou, or soup au giraumon, is a traditional Haitian soup made with pumpkin, including vegetables, plantains, meat, pasta and spices. It is a festive dish, deeply rooted in Haitian identity, the preparation of which promotes social cohesion as well as the feeling of belonging to communities. Originally reserved for slave owners, Haitians appropriated the soup when they gained independence from France, making the dish a symbol of their new-found freedom and an expression of their dignity and resilience. .

Ingredients

  • 15 ml (1 tbsp.) lemon juice
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 10 ml (2 tsp.) chef's spices
  • 10 ml (2 tsp.) chef's spices
  • 30 ml (2 tbsp.) olive oil
  • Onion and garlic salt, to taste
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 white leek, minced
  • 3 green onions, minced
  • 1 pumpkin, about 1.4 kg (3 lb), peeled and grated (about 1.5 liters / 6 cups)
  • 1.5 liters (6 cups) chicken broth, approximately
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cut into large cubes
  • 1 sweet potato, peeled and cut into large cubes (optional)
  • 45 ml (3 tbsp.) chopped flat-leaf parsley

Directions

Step 1

In a bowl, mix the meat with the lemon juice to clean the meat (this is a Haitian tradition). In a colander, rinse the meat under cold water. Drain and pat dry with paper towel.

Step 2

In a mortar, crush the garlic and the chef's spices.

Step 3

Preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.

Step 4

In a large saucepan over medium-high heat, brown the meat, garlic and spices in the oil. Add carrots, celery, leek, onions and squash. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring. Add broth, potatoes and sweet potato, if desired. Bring to a boil. Cover and simmer gently for 35 to 40 minutes or until the meat is tender.

Step 5

Add the parsley. Adjust seasoning.
